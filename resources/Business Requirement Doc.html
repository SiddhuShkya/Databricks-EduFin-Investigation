EduFin Portfolio Risk Analysis

Business Requirements Document

Focus: Execution-based learning
Version: 1.0
Date: February 2026

Business Context

The Situation

EduFin has discovered a ₹12 Crore discrepancy in loan disbursements affecting 15,000+ student accounts over 6 months. A federal compliance audit is scheduled for next month, and the CEO needs concrete answers for an emergency board meeting within 48 hours.

Your Role

You are the Data Analyst assigned to this crisis. The CEO is depending on you to analyze the portfolio data and provide decision-ready insights.

The Challenge

You have access to EduFin's complete database. Your job is to answer specific business questions in 5 sequential phases, each building on the previous analysis.

Recent System Changes: EduFin migrated from legacy Oracle CRM to Salesforce in January 2024. While the data migration was largely successful, the data engineering team has flagged potential timestamp inconsistencies that may require validation during your analysis.

Dataset & Standards

Dataset Structure Reference

Context:

  • Scale: Multi-crore education loan portfolio spanning 4 years (2021-2025)
  • Crisis Indicator: Board has flagged rising default rates exceeding industry norms
  • Data Completeness: Full loan lifecycle data from application to current status
  • Analysis Objective: Quantify crisis severity and identify intervention points
  • Note: Exact portfolio size, default exposure, and financial impact are unknown - you must discover these through SQL analysis.

Available Tables

1. loans.csv (Core Transactional Table)

  • Identifiers: loan_id, customer_id, institution_id
  • Financial: loan_amount, interest_rate, emi_amount
  • Status: loan_status (Active, Defaulted, Overdue, Closed)
  • Timeline: application_date, disbursement_date, maturity_date
  • Details: loan_tenure_months, purpose_of_loan

2. customers.csv (Demographics)

  • Demographics: customer_id, full_name, date_of_birth, gender, education_level
  • Financial: annual_income, cibil_score, employment_type, employer_name
  • Contact: phone_number, email_address, current_address
  • Location: city_id

3. institutions.csv (Partners)

  • Details: institution_id, institution_name, institution_type, tier

4. geographic_demographics.csv & dim_city.csv (Location)

  • City and state details for geographic analysis
  • fiscal_year_start: Company's fiscal year start date (2023-04-01 for FY 2023)

5. defaults_collections.csv & payments.csv (Recovery)

  • Payment history and collection activity logs

Formatting Standards

Currency Display (₹ INR)

  • Crores: Amounts ≥ ₹1,00,00,000 → Display as "₹X.XX Cr"
    • Example: ₹24,240,000 = ₹2.42 Cr (divide by 10,000,000, round to 2 decimals)
  • Lakhs: Amounts < ₹1 Crore → Display as "₹X.XX L"
    • Example: ₹441,000 = ₹4.41 L (divide by 100,000, round to 2 decimals)
  • Use professional formatting with commas for readability in source data

Percentage Precision

  • Default rates and major metrics: 2 decimal places (e.g., 11.00%)
  • Portfolio distributions: 1 decimal place (e.g., 79.3%)
  • Always use ROUND function for consistency

Visual Indicators

  • Revenue Generating: Active loans
  • Successfully Recovered: Closed loans
  • Revenue Loss: Defaulted loans
  • At Risk: Overdue loans

Column Naming

  • Use business-friendly aliases (not database column names)
  • Example: loan_count → "Loan Count", default_rate → "Default Rate %"
  • Capitalize properly for executive readability

Portfolio Health Classification System

Classification Thresholds

Classification Default Rate Range Visual Indicator Required Action
HEALTHY ≤ 5.00% Green Standard monitoring protocols
MODERATE RISK > 5.00% to ≤ 10.00% Orange Enhanced monitoring required
HIGH RISK > 10.00% to ≤ 15.00% Yellow Enhanced collection + review underwriting
CRITICAL > 15.00% Red Crisis team + external recovery agencies

Industry Benchmarks

  • Normal Range: 5-8% default rate for education loan portfolios
  • Warning Threshold: 10% (triggers enhanced intervention)
  • Crisis Threshold: 12% (requires crisis management team)
  • Calculation: (Number of Defaulted Loans ÷ Total Disbursed Loans) × 100

The 5-Phase Analysis

PHASE 1 — Monday | Portfolio Health Check

Business Request from CEO:
"I need to know the scale of our portfolio problem. How big is our loan book? How much have we lost to defaults? What's our overall default rate? Give me the numbers I need to explain to the board how serious this crisis is. Calculate default rate as: (Number of loans with status='Defaulted') / (Total loans disbursed)"

Analytical Approach:

Query 1A: Portfolio Status Distribution

  • Calculate: Count and percentage of loans in each status category (Active, Defaulted, Overdue, Closed)
  • Business Purpose: Understand the composition of the loan book.
  • Key Requirement: Sort by largest category to show primary status immediately.

Query 1B: Basic Portfolio Scale Metrics

  • Calculate: Total unique customers, total loans, total portfolio value, and average loan size.
  • Business Purpose: Establish the scale of operations to justify resource allocation.
  • Formatting: Use INR formatting (Crores/Lakhs).

Query 1C: Risk Category Breakdown

  • Calculate: Counts for each risk category using conditional aggregation.
  • Business Purpose: Quantify specific risk buckets (Defaulted, Overdue) vs. Healthy loans.
  • Key Metrics: Include Default Rate % and Portfolio At Risk % (Defaulted + Overdue).

Query 1D: Financial Impact Assessment

  • Calculate: Total monetary value at risk for each status category.
  • Business Purpose: Translate loan counts into actual financial exposure.
  • Key Requirement: Show "Total At Risk Value" (Defaulted + Overdue amounts).

Query 1E: Executive Dashboard with Health Classification

  • Calculate: A single comprehensive view combining all above metrics.
  • Business Purpose: Board-ready summary with automated insights.
  • Required Columns:
    • Status counts and percentages
    • Financial values in Crores
    • Automated Health Classification (based on thresholds)
    • Recommended Board Action
  • REQUIRED Text Summary: Executive summary interpreting:
    • Severity of default rate vs benchmarks
    • Financial implication of confirmed losses
    • Viability of the active portfolio

PHASE 2 — Tuesday | Geographic Risk Analysis

Business Request from CEO:
"Where are we bleeding money? Which cities have the worst default rates? I need to know which geographic markets are causing this crisis so we can adjust our regional strategies."

Analytical Approach:

Query 2A: Geographic Borrower Distribution

  • Calculate: Number of customers and loans per city.
  • Business Purpose: Identify our largest markets by volume.
  • Filter: Focus on disbursed loans only.

Query 2B: City-Level Risk Metrics

  • Calculate: Default rate per city.
  • Business Purpose: Pinpoint which specific locations are underperforming.
  • Filter: Exclude cities with low volume (<100 loans) to avoid statistical noise.

Query 2C: Financial Exposure by Knowledge Hub

  • Calculate: Total portfolio value and total defaulted value per city.
  • Business Purpose: Identify where the most capital is at risk.
  • Formatting: Convert huge sums to Crores/Lakhs.

Query 2D: Geographic Risk Classification

  • Calculate: Classify cities as 'Critical', 'High Risk', 'Monitor', or 'Safe' based on default rates.
  • Business Purpose: Prioritize regional intervention teams.
  • Rules: defaults > 15% = Critical, > 10% = High Risk.

Query 2E: Regional Crisis Dashboard

  • Calculate: Comprehensive city-level dashboard combining volume, risk, and financial impact.
  • Business Purpose: Strategic decision support for regional shutdowns.
  • Sorting: Sort by "Total Losses" (Defaulted Value) to prioritize highest financial damage.
  • REQUIRED Text Summary: Recommend 3 cities to halt lending in immediately.

PHASE 3 — Wednesday | Customer Risk Segmentation

Note: For our collection workflows, we follow RBI guidelines which define default as loans 90+ days past due, regardless of status field.

Business Request from Collections Manager:
"My collection team needs a prioritized target list. Who are our riskiest customers? I need to know who has multiple defaults, who owes us the most money, and what action we should take."

Analytical Approach:

Query 3A: Customer Profile Basics

  • Calculate: List customers with their basic loan counts and total disbursed amounts.
  • Business Purpose: Build complete borrower profiles.

Query 3B: Default Behavior Analysis

  • Calculate: Identify customers with at least one defaulted loan.
  • Business Purpose: Isolate the problem borrower segment.

Query 3C: High-Value Defaulter Identification

  • Calculate: Total defaulted amount per customer.
  • Business Purpose: Focus collections on high-value recovery targets.
  • Filter: Focus on customers with total default exposure > ₹5 Lakhs.

Query 3D: Demographic Risk Correlation

  • Calculate: Analyze if certain income brackets or employment types have higher default rates.
  • Business Purpose: Refine future underwriting criteria.

Query 3E: Prioritized Collections Target List

  • Calculate: Final ranked list of customers for immediate action.
  • Business Purpose: Daily call list for collections team.
  • Risk Calculation: Assign 'Priority Score' based on Default Amount + CIBIL Score.
  • REQUIRED Text Summary: Profile of the "Ideal High-Risk Borrower" to watch out for.

PHASE 4 — Thursday | Institutional Partnership Risk

Compliance Update: For regulatory reporting, default is defined as loans 180+ days past due OR loans with write-off flag. This differs from operational definitions.

Business Request from Partnership Director:
"Some of our partner institutions are consistently sending us students who default. I need to know which universities and colleges have the worst track records."

Analytical Approach:

Query 4A: Institutional Volume Analysis

  • Calculate: Loan volume and total funding per institution.
  • Business Purpose: Identify key partners by size.

Query 4B: Partner Performance Metrics

  • Calculate: Default rate per university/college.
  • Business Purpose: Measure quality of students from each partner.
  • Filter: Minimum 50 loans to ensure statistical significance.

Query 4C: Financial Loss Attribution

  • Calculate: Total value of defaulted loans attributed to each institution.
  • Business Purpose: Determine which partnerships are costing EduFin money.

Query 4D: Partnership Tier & Risk Classification

  • Calculate: Categorize partners (Preferred vs. Probation vs. Blacklist).
  • Business Purpose: Automated partnership review status.
  • Rules: Default rate > 15% = Blacklist Candidate.

Query 4E: Partnership Review Dashboard

  • Calculate: Comprehensive partner scorecard.
  • Business Purpose: Support contract renegotiation or termination.
  • Sorting: Highest Default Rate first.
  • REQUIRED Text Summary: List of top 5 institutions to blacklist immediately.

PHASE 5 — Friday | Crisis Timeline + Time Intelligence

Business Request from CEO:
"When did this crisis start? Was it sudden or gradual? I need Q4 2023 default trends for the board meeting next week."

Analytical Approach:

Query 5A: Monthly Disbursement Trends

  • Calculate: Total loans and amount disbursed per month (last 24 months).
  • Business Purpose: Visualize growth trajectory.

Query 5B: Tracking Defaults by Vintage

  • Calculate: How many loans from each month's cohort eventually defaulted.
  • Business Purpose: Cohort analysis to pinpoint bad vintage months.

Query 5C: Monthly Default Rate Trends

  • Calculate: Default Rate % for each month.
  • Business Purpose: The core "Crisis Timeline" metric.

Query 5D: Cumulative Financial Impact

  • Calculate: Rolling total of losses over time.
  • Business Purpose: See how the financial hole grew.

Query 5E: Crisis Timeline Dashboard

  • Calculate: Month-by-month crisis heat map.
  • Business Purpose: Pinpoint the exact "Point of Failure" (month where defaults spiked).
  • Classifications: CRISIS MONTH (>15%), WARNING MONTH (10-15%).
  • REQUIRED Text Summary: Identify the exact month the crisis began and whether it is currently stabilizing or worsening.

🔧 Query 5F: Month-over-Month Default Velocity (Custom Column Required)

  • Calculate: MOM change in default rate using LAG() window function.
  • Custom Column: default_velocity — label each month as Accelerating / Stable / Improving.
  • Trap: The first month has no prior period. Return 'Baseline' — not NULL.
  • Trap: The denominator changes each month as new loans are added. Document which denominator you used.

🔧 Query 5G: Cohort Performance Analysis (Custom Column Required)

  • Calculate: Tag each loan by disbursement quarter (e.g. '2022-Q3'). Calculate default rate per cohort.
  • Custom Column: cohort_label — identify the worst-performing cohort by name.
  • Business Purpose: Did EduFin's underwriting standards worsen in a specific period?
  • Trap: A cohort with fewer than 50 loans is statistically insufficient — flag it, don't rank it.

Query 5H: Crisis Synthesis (Friday Capstone — Phase 1 + Phase 5 Combined)

  • Question: Using your Phase 1 financial totals and your Phase 5 crisis onset date — estimate: what would total defaulted exposure have been at the moment the portfolio first crossed 10%?
  • This cannot be answered by one query alone. It requires combining Phase 1 and Phase 5 outputs with written reasoning.
  • REQUIRED: Show your working. State your assumptions explicitly.
  • AI cannot do this correctly without you providing the Phase 1 context. This is the Week 1 capstone question.

Out of Scope

This analysis does NOT require:

  • Dashboard creation (Tableau/PowerBI)
  • Predictive modeling (Machine Learning)
  • Customer communication templates
  • Process redesign recommendations

Focus: Answer the specific business questions with SQL data.

Progressive Difficulty — What Each Day Builds

  • Phase 1 (Mon): Portfolio-level aggregation + threshold classification + your first custom column judgment call
  • Phase 2 (Tue): Multi-table JOINs at city grain + rate × exposure combined ranking (the analyst vs. query-writer distinction)
  • Phase 3 (Wed): Customer-level segmentation using multiple signals + action-mapped output (collections strategy, not a list)
  • Phase 4 (Thu): Partner risk with statistical discipline + legal-grade justification writing
  • Phase 5 (Fri): Time intelligence (LAG, cohort analysis, MOM trend) + cross-phase synthesis question

Cumulative effect: By Friday, the output of every phase connects. Phase 1 context is needed for the Phase 5 synthesis. That is how real analysis works.

Submission Standard — What "Done" Looks Like

A completed phase submission has three parts, not one:

  • 1. Your SQL — The queries. Any tool (SQLite, Python, online SQL editor).
  • 2. Your Custom Column — At least one column you built that the BRD did not ask for. Include the column name, CASE logic, and a one-sentence justification for your threshold choices.
  • 3. Your Business Recommendation — 3 sentences max. Written to the stakeholder named in that phase's CEO brief. Not a query result — a decision statement with a specific action.

Before submitting: Answer the three WHY questions at the end of each phase in the Logic Guide. If you cannot answer them — your analysis is not ready.

AI note: AI tools can generate the SQL. They cannot justify your custom column thresholds, write your specific business recommendation, or answer why your denominator choice matters for this portfolio. Those are yours.

Ready? Classify the schema first. Then start Phase 1.
